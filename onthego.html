Homeschooling on the Go: Our Journey with My Daughters

Posted on November 15, 2023

As a mother of two beautiful girls, aged 1 and 7, I’ve found that homeschooling is not just about education—it’s about creating a life rich with experiences, curiosity, and creativity.

Travel as Our Classroom

Travel has become the heart of our homeschooling journey. For my 1-year-old, even a trip to the local park is an adventure filled with new sights, sounds, and textures. For my 7-year-old, travel opens up a world of deeper learning and discovery.

  • Theater and Dance Shows: One of our favorite things to do is attend live performances. Whether it’s a small community theater production or a professional ballet, these experiences spark my 7-year-old’s imagination and inspire her to create her own stories and dances. Even my 1-year-old is captivated by the colors, music, and movement.
  • Museums and Cultural Sites: We love exploring museums and historical sites. For my 7-year-old, I often create little scavenger hunts or ask her to pick her favorite exhibit and tell me why she loves it. These outings are not just educational—they’re bonding moments that we’ll cherish forever.

Multilingual Adventures

Coming from a multilingual background, language has always been a big part of our family life. I want my girls to grow up fluent in our heritage languages while also mastering English.

  • Daily Language Practice: With my 1-year-old, I sing songs and read simple books in different languages. It’s amazing how quickly she picks up words and phrases! For my 7-year-old, we do more structured language lessons, but I also encourage her to write stories or letters in different languages. She loves creating her own bilingual books.
  • Language Through Travel: When we travel, I make it a point to practice the languages we speak. For example, if we visit a country where one of our heritage languages is spoken, I encourage my 7-year-old to talk to locals or read signs. She keeps a travel journal where she writes in different languages, and it’s been incredible to see her confidence grow.

Art-Infused Curriculum

Art is a huge part of our lives. I believe it’s essential for nurturing creativity and self-expression, and I’ve made it a priority to incorporate fine arts into our homeschooling.

  • Art Projects: My 1-year-old loves finger painting and playing with clay. It’s messy, but seeing her explore colors and textures is worth every wipe-down. My 7-year-old is more into drawing, painting, and even trying her hand at simple sculptures. We often work on art projects together, and it’s a wonderful way to connect.
  • Art Appreciation: We visit art galleries and exhibitions whenever we can. I love asking my 7-year-old what she thinks about the artworks we see. Sometimes, she’ll recreate her favorite pieces at home, adding her own twist. It’s amazing to see how art inspires her creativity./li>

Learning in Living Color: How Everyday Moments Become Our Greatest Lessons

One of the things I love most about homeschooling is the flexibility it gives us. We don’t just learn at a desk—we learn through life.

  • Routine and Play: While we have a loose routine, I try to keep things flexible. For my 1-year-old, learning happens through play. We sing, dance, and explore the world together. For my 7-year-old, I incorporate more structured activities, but I also leave plenty of room for her to pursue her interests.
  • Project-Based Learning: My 7-year-old loves projects. Recently, she created a travel scrapbook after one of our trips, complete with drawings, photos, and captions in different languages. She’s also been working on a short play that she wants to perform for the family. It’s incredible to see how much she learns through these self-directed projects.

Survival Kit for Homeschool Explorers

Every adventurer needs supplies! Here’s my treasure trove of resources and fellow travelers who’ve kept us afloat.

  • Online Resources: We use educational websites, virtual museum tours, and language apps to supplement our learning. There’s so much out there, and it’s been a lifesaver on days when we need a little extra inspiration.
  • Local Communities: We’ve joined a local homeschooling group, and it’s been wonderful for both my girls. My 7-year-old loves the social aspect, and it’s great for me to connect with other parents who are on the same journey.

Conclusion:

Homeschooling my daughters has been one of the most rewarding experiences of my life. By focusing on travel, language arts, and fine arts, I’ve been able to create a learning environment that feels natural, joyful, and deeply meaningful. Whether we’re exploring a new city, attending a dance performance, or painting together at home, every day is an opportunity to learn and grow together. I’m so grateful for this journey and the chance to watch my girls flourish in their own unique ways. Here’s to many more adventures ahead!
